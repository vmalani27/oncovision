Types of Skin Cancer

Malignant

Melanoma (mel)

Melanoma is the most serious type of skin cancer. It develops from melanocytes, the cells that produce melanin. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.

Characteristics:
  • Asymmetrical shape
  • Irregular borders
  • Varied colors
  • Diameter larger than 6mm
  • Evolving appearance

Basal Cell Carcinoma (bcc)

BCC is the most common type of skin cancer. It typically appears as a waxy bump or flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion.

Characteristics:
  • Pearly or waxy bump
  • Flat, flesh-colored lesion
  • Brown scar-like lesion
  • Slow-growing

Pre-cancerous

Actinic Keratosis (akiec)

Also known as solar keratosis, this is a pre-cancerous growth that can develop into squamous cell carcinoma if left untreated.

Characteristics:
  • Rough, scaly patches
  • Usually less than 2cm
  • Often appears on sun-exposed areas
  • May be pink or red

Benign

Benign Keratosis (bkl)

A common non-cancerous skin growth that typically appears as a brown, black, or light tan growth.

Characteristics:
  • Round or oval shape
  • Well-defined borders
  • Usually uniform color
  • Slow-growing

Melanocytic Nevi (nv)

Common moles that are usually harmless but should be monitored for changes.

Characteristics:
  • Round or oval shape
  • Uniform color
  • Usually smaller than 6mm
  • May be flat or raised

Dermatofibroma (df)

A common benign skin growth that typically appears as a firm, raised growth.

Characteristics:
  • Firm to the touch
  • Usually pink or brown
  • Often appears on legs
  • May dimple when pinched

Important Note

While our AI model can help identify potential skin conditions, it's important to:

  • Consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis
  • Regularly examine your skin for changes
  • Protect your skin from excessive sun exposure
  • Schedule regular skin cancer screenings
