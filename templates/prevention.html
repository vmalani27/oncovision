Preventing Skin Cancer

Sun Protection Guidelines

Daily Protection
  • Use broad-spectrum sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Apply sunscreen 15-30 minutes before sun exposure
  • Reapply every 2 hours or after swimming
  • Use water-resistant sunscreen when swimming
Additional Measures
  • Seek shade during peak sun hours (10 AM - 4 PM)
  • Wear protective clothing
  • Use wide-brimmed hats
  • Wear UV-blocking sunglasses

Healthy Habits

  • Regular skin self-examinations
  • Annual professional skin checks
  • Maintain a healthy diet rich in antioxidants
  • Stay hydrated
  • Exercise regularly
  • Get adequate sleep

Avoid These

  • Tanning beds and sunlamps
  • Excessive sun exposure
  • Sunburns
  • Smoking
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Unprotected sun exposure

Early Detection Signs

ABCDE Rule for Melanoma
  • Asymmetry: Irregular shape
  • Border: Uneven or jagged edges
  • Color: Multiple colors or shades
  • Diameter: Larger than 6mm
  • Evolving: Changes in size, shape, or color
Common Warning Signs
  • New growth on the skin
  • Change in existing mole
  • Non-healing sore
  • Red or scaly patch
  • Itching or tenderness
When to See a Doctor
  • Any new skin growth
  • Changes in existing moles
  • Non-healing sores
  • Unusual skin changes
  • Family history of skin cancer

Important Reminder

While these prevention measures can significantly reduce your risk of skin cancer, it's important to:

  • Consult healthcare professionals for personalized advice
  • Maintain regular skin cancer screenings
  • Be aware of your family history
  • Stay informed about new prevention methods
